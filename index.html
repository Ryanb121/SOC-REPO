Sir David Attenborough

English broadcaster, writer, and naturalist

Here is a time line of Attenborough's life

  • 1926 - born on May 8th 1926 in London, England.
  • 1947 - Graduates from Clare College, Cambridge, with a degree in Natural Sciences.
  • 1952 - 1952 – Joined the BBC as a trainee producer.
  • 1957 - Forms the BBC’s Travel and Exploration Unit.
  • 1969 - Promoted to BBC Director of Programmes, overseeing all BBC TV output.
  • 1985 - Knighted by HRH Queen Elizabeth II for services to factual broadcasting.
  • 1998 - Writes and presents The Life of Birds.
  • 2005 - Life in the Undergrowth, the series was one of the first to use cutting edge macro filming techniques to film insect life and behaviour.
  • 2009 - Narrates Life and presents Charles Darwin and the Tree of Life.
  • 2014 - David Attenborough’s Natural History Museum Alive.
  • 2016 - Planet Earth II: Sir David presents a new landmark series that sets out to reveal our planet from a completely new perspective, using the significant advances in both filming technology and our understanding of the natural world

"After studying the natural sciences at the University of Cambridge, broadcaster David Attenborough began his career as a producer at the BBC, where he launched the successful Zoo Quest series. Attenborough was made controller of BBC Two in 1965 and later was named its director of programming. During his tenure, the station crossed over to color television, and Attenborough was instrumental in expanding its natural history content. Attenborough left the BBC to begin writing and producing various series, including the smash hit Life on Earth, which set the standards for the modern nature documentary. Since then Attenborough has written, produced, hosted and narrated countless award-winning, nature-focused programs and has devoted his life to celebrating and preserving wildlife."

-- David Blatty
